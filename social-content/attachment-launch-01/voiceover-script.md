# English Voiceover Script

## Final 15-Second Script

Why do you pull away when things get real?

It may not be coldness. Sometimes distance is a protection pattern.

Take the free attachment style test, and see what yours may be protecting.

## Timing

- `0.3–3.2s` — “Why do you pull away when things get real?”
- `3.3–8.6s` — “It may not be coldness. Sometimes distance is a protection pattern.”
- `8.8–14.4s` — “Take the free attachment style test, and see what yours may be protecting.”
- `14.4–15.0s` — Leave the final CTA visible without speech.

## Voice Direction

- Natural conversational English, not a commercial announcer voice.
- Warm, calm and slightly reflective.
- Speak as if sharing an observation with one person.
- Target `132–138 words per minute`.
- Use a small pause after “coldness.”
- Gently emphasize “protection pattern” and “free attachment style test.”
- Do not sound overly soothing, mystical, dramatic or therapeutic.
- Keep natural breaths; do not remove every pause.

## Preferred Recording

The most authentic result is a real human recording made on a phone or USB microphone in a quiet, soft room.

- Record as WAV or high-quality M4A.
- Keep the microphone approximately 15–20 cm away.
- Record two versions: one neutral and one slightly more personal.
- Do not add background music during recording.

## If Synthetic Speech Is Used

- Choose a warm adult voice with restrained emotion.
- Avoid perfect timing and exaggerated sentence endings.
- Slightly vary pacing between sentences.
- Add only light compression and noise reduction.
- Review the entire render before publishing; replace it if it sounds synthetic at normal phone volume.
